# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

The stop-wasting-tokens (SWT) community is committed to providing a welcoming, respectful, and productive space for everyone — regardless of experience level, identity, or background. We want this project to be a place where contributors can ask questions, file friction reports, propose ideas, and ship work without friction unrelated to the work itself.

## Our standard

We adopt the [Contributor Covenant 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) as our community standard. The full text of expected behavior, prohibited behavior, enforcement responsibilities, and enforcement guidelines is available at the linked URL.

In short:

- **Treat people as collaborators**, not adversaries. Disagree about ideas, not identities.
- **Receive feedback gracefully** and offer it constructively.
- **Assume good intent**, but address impact when it falls short.
- **Respect time and attention** — be concise, link to context, and avoid pile-ons.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces — the GitHub repo (issues, pull requests, discussions), the Discord server, official social media, and any other channel where someone is participating in the SWT community on behalf of the project.

## Reporting

If you witness or experience behavior that falls below the standard, please report it privately:

- Email: `conduct@stopwastingtokens.dev` _(placeholder — replace with the real address before public beta)_
- Or DM a maintainer on Discord

Reports will be handled confidentially. Maintainers will follow the [Contributor Covenant 2.1 enforcement guidelines](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/#enforcement-guidelines), which lay out the four-step ladder (correction → warning → temporary ban → permanent ban) based on severity and history.

## Maintainer commitment

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying expectations, taking corrective action when needed, and applying these guidelines fairly. Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct may be removed from their role.
